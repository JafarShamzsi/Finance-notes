# Essential Books

Ranked by importance for a quant at JPM/Bloomberg level. Start with tier 1.

---

## Tier 1: Must Read

### Algorithmic Trading & Strategies
| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Algorithmic Trading** | Ernest Chan | Practical, code-heavy, covers mean reversion and momentum |
| **Quantitative Trading** | Ernest Chan | Starting a quant trading business, strategy evaluation |
| **Advances in Financial Machine Learning** | Marcos López de Prado | Modern ML for finance, triple barrier, meta-labeling, HRP |
| **Trading and Exchanges** | Larry Harris | Market microstructure bible, how markets really work |

### Mathematics & Statistics
| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives** | John Hull | Derivatives bible, every quant desk has this |
| **Paul Wilmott Introduces Quantitative Finance** | Paul Wilmott | Accessible quant finance math |
| **Analysis of Financial Time Series** | Ruey Tsay | Time series for finance, GARCH, VAR models |

### Risk & Portfolio
| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Active Portfolio Management** | Grinold & Kahn | The fundamental law of active management, IR, alpha |
| **Risk Budgeting** | Roncalli | Modern portfolio construction, risk parity |

---

## Tier 2: Highly Recommended

### Strategy & Alpha
| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Machine Learning for Asset Managers** | López de Prado | Practical ML for portfolio construction |
| **Pairs Trading** | Ganapathy Vidyamurthy | Deep dive into stat arb and pairs |
| **Momentum and Mean Reversion** | Tim Bollerslev et al. | Academic treatment of core strategies |
| **Inside the Black Box** | Rishi Narang | How quant funds actually work |
| **Finding Alphas** | Igor Tulchinsky (WorldQuant) | Alpha generation, 101 formulaic alphas |

### Market Microstructure
| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Market Microstructure Theory** | Maureen O'Hara | Theoretical foundations |
| **Algorithmic and High-Frequency Trading** | Cartea, Jaimungal, Penalva | Math of optimal execution and HFT |
| **Flash Boys** | Michael Lewis | Narrative on HFT (not technical but important context) |

### Programming
| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Python for Finance** | Yves Hilpisch | Python financial computing |
| **Python for Data Analysis** | Wes McKinney | Pandas creator's guide |

## Tier 3: Deep Dives

| Book | Author | Topic |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Stochastic Calculus for Finance I & II** | Steven Shreve | Rigorous stochastic calc |
| **The Concepts and Practice of Mathematical Finance** | Mark Joshi | Derivatives pricing |
| **Quantitative Risk Management** | McNeil, Frey, Embrechts | VaR, EVT, copulas |
| **Statistical Arbitrage** | Andrew Pole | Stat arb techniques |
| **Market Making and Mean Reversion** | Foucault, Pagano, Röell | Market design theory |
| **Optimal Trading Strategies** | Robert Kissell | Execution algorithms |
| **Dark Pools** | Scott Patterson | History of electronic trading |
| **The Man Who Solved the Market** | Gregory Zuckerman | Jim Simons and Renaissance Technologies |

---

## Reading Order for New Quants
1. Chan — *Quantitative Trading* (practical intro)
2. Harris — *Trading and Exchanges* (market structure)
3. Hull — *Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives* (derivatives)
4. Chan — *Algorithmic Trading* (implementation)
5. López de Prado — *Advances in Financial ML* (modern methods)
6. Grinold & Kahn — *Active Portfolio Management* (portfolio construction)
