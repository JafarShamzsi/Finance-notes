# Connectivity

Connectivity refers to the way in which a trading server is connected to exchanges and brokers. The quality and reliability of the connection is critical for the success of any trading strategy.

## Types of Connectivity

### Internet
- **Description:** The most common and least expensive type of connectivity.
- **Pros:** Inexpensive, widely available.
- **Cons:** High latency, unreliable.

### VPN (Virtual Private Network)
- **Description:** A VPN can be used to create a secure and reliable connection over the internet.
- **Pros:** More secure and reliable than a public internet connection.
- **Cons:** Higher latency than a direct connection.

### Direct Connection
- **Description:** A dedicated physical connection to an exchange or broker.
- **Pros:** Low latency, high reliability.
- **Cons:** Expensive, not widely available.

## Cross-Connects
- **Description:** A physical connection between two servers in the same data center.
- **Importance:** Cross-connects are used to connect a trading server to an exchange's matching engine in a colocation facility. This is the lowest latency form of connectivity available.
