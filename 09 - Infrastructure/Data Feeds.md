# Data Feeds

A data feed is a stream of real-time or historical data that is used to power trading algorithms. The quality and reliability of the data feed is critical for the success of any trading strategy.

## Types of Data Feeds

### Real-Time Data Feeds
- **Description:** Provide a continuous stream of market data, such as quotes, trades, and order book updates.
- **Protocols:**
    - **FIX (Financial Information eXchange):** A widely used protocol for real-time market data and trade execution.
    - **ITCH:** A protocol used by NASDAQ for disseminating market data.
    - **Proprietary Protocols:** Many exchanges and brokers have their own proprietary data feed protocols.

### Historical Data
- **Description:** Provides a record of past market data.
- **Sources:**
    - **Data Vendors:** Companies like Bloomberg and Refinitiv provide high-quality historical data.
    - **Exchanges:** Exchanges provide historical data for their own products.
    - **Free Sources:** There are many free sources of historical data available online, but the quality can be variable.

## Data Quality
- **Cleanliness:** The data should be free of errors and outliers.
- **Completeness:** The data should be complete, with no missing data points.
- **Timeliness:** Real-time data should be delivered with low latency.
- **Adjustments:** Historical data should be adjusted for corporate actions, such as stock splits and dividends.
