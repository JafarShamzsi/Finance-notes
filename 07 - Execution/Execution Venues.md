# Execution Venues

An execution venue is a marketplace where securities are traded. The choice of execution venue can have a significant impact on the quality of trade execution, including the price, speed, and likelihood of execution.

## Types of Execution Venues

### Lit Venues (Exchanges)
- **Description:** Lit venues, such as the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and NASDAQ, display pre-trade transparency in the form of a public order book.
- **Pros:** Price discovery, transparency.
- **Cons:** Information leakage, potential for market impact.

### Dark Venues (Dark Pools)
- **Description:** Dark pools are private exchanges or forums for trading securities. They do not display pre-trade transparency.
- **Pros:** Reduced market impact, potential for price improvement.
- **Cons:** Lack of transparency, potential for adverse selection.

## Factors to Consider When Choosing a Venue
- **Liquidity:** The amount of trading activity in a particular security.
- **Fees:** The costs associated with trading on a particular venue.
- **Speed:** The speed at which orders are executed.
- **Order Types:** The types of orders that are supported by the venue.
- **Market Data:** The quality and cost of market data feeds.
